# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

The `frsutils` community is committed to a respectful, inclusive, and
professional environment for researchers, developers, reviewers, and users of
all backgrounds and experience levels.

## Expected behavior

Community members are expected to:

- communicate with patience, clarity, and respect;
- discuss ideas, evidence, code, and scientific claims rather than attacking
  people;
- welcome questions and acknowledge that contributors have different levels of
  familiarity with fuzzy-rough sets and scientific Python;
- give constructive, specific feedback and accept correction gracefully;
- disclose relevant limitations, uncertainty, and conflicts of interest;
- respect privacy, attribution, licensing, and research integrity.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

- harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or personal insults;
- sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention;
- deliberate disruption, trolling, or repeated bad-faith argument;
- publishing another person's private information without permission;
- plagiarism, fabricated evidence, or knowingly misleading scientific claims;
- retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith.

## Scope

This policy applies in the repository, issue tracker, pull requests, code review,
documentation, project-related email, conferences, and other public or private
spaces where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainer at
`meam64@gmail.com`. Include relevant links, screenshots, dates, and context when
possible. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially and promptly as practical.
The maintainer may edit or remove content, issue a warning, temporarily restrict
participation, or permanently ban participation depending on the severity and
history of the behavior.

Anyone handling a report must avoid conflicts of interest. If a report concerns
the current maintainer, the reporter may ask a trusted neutral researcher or
open-source community moderator to help document and mediate the matter.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the Contributor Covenant, version 2.1, and adapted to
the needs of a small scientific open-source project.
